I'm refining the strategic scope of Reserve Tāmaki's work based on our Tāmaki Ora funding framework and the reality of economic development in our Māori and Pasifika community.

CONTEXT:
Reserve Tāmaki is an innovation hub in Tāmaki, Auckland supporting Māori and Pasifika economic agency and entrepreneurship. We've been focused primarily on "business" support, but we're expanding our scope to better align with:

1. Our Tāmaki Ora funding (Māori Outcomes Fund from Auckland Council)
2. The actual ventures our community is building
3. A broader definition of economic wellbeing

KEY SHIFT:
From: Supporting only traditional businesses (registered companies with revenue)
To: Supporting diverse ventures that create economic agency - including commercial businesses, social enterprises, creative movements, cultural collectives, and community-led initiatives

VENTURE TYPES WE NOW SUPPORT:
1. Commercial Businesses - registered companies, trading, revenue-generating
2. Social Enterprises - purpose-driven, some revenue, reinvest profit into mission
3. Creative/Cultural Movements - brand/content driven, income through sponsorship/grants/partnerships (e.g., street athletes calisthenics collective)
4. Passion Projects Transitioning - building toward sustainability, testing models

OUR FUNDING ALIGNMENT (Tāmaki Ora):
We contribute to three outcome areas:
- Whai Rawa Ora (Economic Wellbeing) - Māori businesses, entrepreneurs, economic agency
- Huatau Ora (Future Wellbeing) - Digital innovation, rangatahi pathways, intergenerational knowledge
- Te Hapori Ora (Community Wellbeing) - Whānau connected, community initiatives, rangatahi engagement

REAL EXAMPLE:
Four young Māori guys (18-23) passionate about calisthenics. They call themselves "street athletes" and want to build a movement. They're developing their brand, creating content, building community, and exploring sponsorship/partnership models. This ISN'T a traditional business, but it IS economic development - they're building digital capability, brand strategy, community leadership, and pathways to income.
