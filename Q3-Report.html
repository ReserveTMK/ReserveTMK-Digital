4. Updates

Content & Media

  • Kimani Ngaluafe (Pass Me The Ball) delivered his first ever workshop as a facilitator — a creator session for rangatahi focused on content and camera skills. He has since landed his first paying client and earns consistently through content monetisation.
  • Podcast studio generating organic collaboration — Zac Moran (Manaiakalani) now working with Thomas Morsby (Five Rings Media) on his podcast.
  • Te Rina Triponel (Tide Talks) developing a podcast series on authentic young urban Māori stories, supported through space access and mentorship.
  • Gear access growing — Benji, Kimani, and Adhi regularly using cameras and audio equipment. Eagles pressed their first merchandise run using the office space.

Rangatahi & Community Activation

  • Level Up (rangatahi group) used the hub twice this quarter to workshop their campaign for a calisthenics park in Glen Innes — facilitated by Kimani, 9–10 young Māori rangatahi using the space as their base for community advocacy.

Mentoring & Pathways

  • John Tanuvasa (OHN Clothing) reached a two-year milestone — arriving now self-directed, using the hub as a check-in rather than a guide.
  • Theresa (Wāhine entrepreneur) progressing towards her vision of a neurodivergent café-play space for East Auckland families, referred through Foundation North.
  • Cleona Parata (Relax & Reset Gifts) building her product business online, session by session.

Ecosystem & Partnerships

  • Aysha Peters (Maia Collective) formalised a referral pathway for wāhine Māori entrepreneurs into our space.
  • New relationship with Tāmaki Primary's principal — STEM workshops in development for Q4.
  • Tyrone Tangata-Makiri (Second Half) confirmed for two AI workshops in April.

Whanaungatanga Highlights

  • Tāmaki Primary connection opens a direct pipeline between Reserve Tāmaki and the local school community.
  • Maia Collective partnership reflects growing trust between aligned kaupapa Māori organisations.
  • Roof Hub has embedded a weekly Monday meeting rhythm — demonstrating the hub's value as a professional base for local organisations.
