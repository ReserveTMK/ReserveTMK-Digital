## Auckland Council EDO

**Full name:** Auckland Council Economic Development Office
**Agreement:** $75,000 + GST, FY26 (1 Jul 2025 – 30 Jun 2026)
**History:** Previously Tātaki Auckland Unlimited (TAU), an external CCO. Absorbed back into Council, became EDO. Ran the GridAKL entrepreneurship hub network. Reserve Tāmaki was part of that network.
**Reporting:** Monthly, due 2 days before month end. Structured data table + narrative, PDF output.
**Key personnel:**
- Liaison: Catriona Stewart (catriona.stewart@aucklandcouncil.govt.nz)
- Head: Pam Ford

**What they want:** Clean economic activity numbers. Activations, venue hires, foot traffic, capability building. Catriona is Ra's internal champion — she takes the reports and presents them in aggregated hub reports alongside GridAKL, Te Ngahere, Te Puna. Reserve Tāmaki had the best numbers for its size last year. Keep that edge.

**Narrative style:** Compliance — numbers first, narrative second. Monthly reports are the primary output.

**Outcomes framework:** Economic Development — business capability, entrepreneurship support, activation of underutilised spaces.

**Priority sections:** delivery, engagement, value, reach

**Partnership strategy:** Monthly reporting gives Catriona ammunition for her combined hub report. Clean, comparable numbers. Reserve Tāmaki positions as best value for size. Reporting feeds directly into Council's EDO performance metrics. Relationship is operational — regular reporting, light touch.

## Ngā Mātārae (Māori Outcomes Fund)

**Full name:** Auckland Council Ngā Mātārae Māori Outcomes Fund
**Organisation:** Auckland Council (Māori Outcomes department, separate from EDO)
**Agreement:** $600,000 over 3 years (2024–2027), $200k/year, $50k/quarter
**History:** Always been Auckland Council proper (not TAU/CCO). When Tātaki was absorbed, Māori Outcomes inherited the economic hubs — didn't choose to, was landed with them during restructure. This means MO needs convincing the investment delivers on THEIR framework, not just "economic development."
**Reporting:** Quarterly progress reports. Primary audience for the big quarterly report.
**Key personnel:**
- Head: Nick Turoa
- Other contacts: Daniel Haines (Māori Outcomes Lead), Marina Matthews

**What they want:** Māori numbers and change. Māori businesses registered, programmes delivered, rangatahi participating, stage progressions. It's a numbers game — they need to justify the investment internally. Direct message: get more Māori business.

**Demographic reality:** Reserve Tāmaki community is under 20% Māori. Pasifika deliberately added as key deliverable. The Māori community in Tāmaki is younger, less educated, poorer — significant lift needed before "Māori business success" metrics show. Pipeline/foundation metrics (Kakano stage) matter more than end-state metrics right now.

**Ecosystem model:** Ecosystem Lifecycle Model — Emergence > Activation > Integration > Maturity. Reserve Tāmaki is at "Emergence" stage, which gives permission to report pipeline metrics, not just end-state outcomes.

**Nick's guidance:** Hyper-local is fine, but show Māori ecosystem connections — Ngāti Pāoa (mana whenua), Ruapōtaka Marae, local Māori networks. "We support X Māori individuals" is weaker than "We are embedded in the Tāmaki Māori ecosystem."

**Narrative style:** Partnership — show capability building, stage progression, ecosystem embeddedness. Not just compliance numbers.

**Outcomes framework:** Tāmaki Ora — Māori capability, community leadership, self-determination. Upskill Māori innovators/entrepreneurs, rangatahi entrepreneurial mindset, visible Tāmaki innovation ecosystem.

**Priority sections:** impact, outcomes, reach, tamaki_ora, cohort

**Partnership strategy:** Quarterly reports lead with Māori-specific numbers and stage progression. Use Emergence framing to justify pipeline metrics. Always show direction of travel (change), not just snapshot. Reports should reference Māori ecosystem connections (iwi, marae, networks), not just individual Māori metrics. Space Use highlights Māori organisations. After this 3-year round ends Jun 2027, Reserve Tāmaki will bid competitively — every report from now until then builds the case. Nick wants direct relationship (bypass EDO) — the quarterly reporting pathway is building toward that.

**Political context:** After Jun 2027, competitive bid. Nick is the future funder relationship. Catriona is the champion now. Both need to be served but MO language should strengthen each quarter.
